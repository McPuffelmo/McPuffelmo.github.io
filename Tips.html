SHORT TIPS!

Stick to designated trails and respect park rules to protect wildlife and fragile ecosystems.

Research your park and check its official website or app for up-to-date info on fees, reservations, and alerts before you go.

Make reservations early for campsites, tours, or permits, especially during peak seasons.

Invest in an annual pass if you plan to visit multiple parks-it can save money on entrance fees.

Pack for all weather conditions; bring layers, rain gear, and sun protection, as park weather can change quickly.

Bring plenty of water, snacks, and a reusable water bottle to stay hydrated and energized.

Visit the park's visitor center for maps, trail updates, and ranger advice.

Store food properly and follow wildlife safety guidelines, especially in bear country.

Travel with a buddy or group and keep everyone together, especially on hikes.

Take advantage of ranger-led programs or guided hikes-they offer expert insights and can help you discover hidden gems within the park.

Visit during the shoulder season or midweek to enjoy fewer crowds and a more peaceful experience.

Leave no trace: pack out all trash and respect the natural environment for future visitors.
