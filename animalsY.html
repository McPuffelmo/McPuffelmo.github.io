Many animals live in Yellowstone Nationalpark

Here's a small list to name a few.


  • Bisons
    are massive animals with thick, woolly coats, a large hump over their shoulders, and curved horns. They live in herds, moving together for protection and social interaction. Bisons are herbivores, spending much of their day grazing on grasses and sedges.


  • Moose
    are the largest members of the deer family, easily recognized by their broad, flat antlers and long legs. They are mostly solitary animals that thrive in forests and wetlands, feeding on leaves, twigs, and aquatic plants. Despite their size, moose are excellent swimmers and can move quietly through dense vegetation.

  • Mountain lions
    are sleek, powerful cats with tawny brown fur, long legs, and a distinctive long tail that helps them balance. They are solitary and territorial predators, primarily hunting deer by stalking silently before pouncing. Adapted to a variety of habitats, mountain lions prefer dense cover or rugged terrain where they can use their stealth and agility to ambush prey.

  • Wolves
    are highly social animals known for their strong pack structure and coordinated hunting strategies. They have thick fur that varies in color from gray to white, black, or brown, helping them blend into their environment. Wolves primarily feed on large ungulates but are also opportunistic, eating smaller animals and carrion when available.
