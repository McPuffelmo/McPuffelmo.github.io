Grand Canyon National Park, established in 1919, is one of the oldest and most iconic national parks in the United States, spanning over 1,900 square miles in northern Arizona. The park is renowned for the Grand Canyon itself, a breathtaking gorge carved by the Colorado River over millions of years, stretching 277 miles long, up to 18 miles wide, and over a mile deep. The canyon's layered red rock formations reveal a rich geological history that dates back nearly two billion years. Grand Canyon National Park is home to diverse wildlife, including bighorn sheep, mule deer, California condors, and numerous reptile and bird species. Visitors can experience the park's natural beauty through hiking, rafting, camping, and scenic viewpoints like the South Rim and North Rim. Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, Grand Canyon National Park stands as a testament to the power of natural forces and remains a must-see destination for travelers from around the globe.
