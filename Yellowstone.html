Yellowstone National Park, established in 1872, is the first national park in the world and covers over 3,400 square miles across Wyoming, Montana, and Idaho. The park is famous for its geothermal features, including more than 500 active geysers, with Old Faithful being the most well-known. Yellowstone sits on a massive supervolcano called the Yellowstone Caldera, which has shaped much of the parkâ€™s unique landscape. It is home to a rich variety of wildlife, such as bison, grizzly bears, wolves, and elk, making it a vital sanctuary for many species. Visitors can enjoy hiking, camping, fishing, and exploring stunning natural wonders like Yellowstone Lake and the Grand Prismatic Spring. As a UNESCO World Heritage Site, Yellowstone remains one of the most treasured natural areas in the world.
